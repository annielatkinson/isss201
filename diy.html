How I Fell in Love with Skiing

I've been skiing for a decade now, starting with lessons from my dad at Meows ski resort on Mount Hood, Oregon. The snow there isn't always ideal, often icy, but from my first time on the slopes, I was hooked. Throughout high school, despite the two-hour drive every weekend, I made it a ritual to ski. The mountain became my sanctuary, a place to clear my head and escape stress. That's why I aimed for schools in Utah, ultimately choosing BYU due to a scholarship offer and the allure of skiing at Sundance. The snow in Utah exceeds all my expectations.

Places I have skiied

  1. Timberline, OR
    • First lift constructed in 1938. It was the second chirlift in North America.
    • Featured as the exterior for the Overlook Hotel in the legendary movie The Shining.
  2. Mt. Hood Meadows, OR
    • Second largest resort in Oregon.
    • 67 miles East of Portlan Oregon.
  3. Mt. Hood Skibowl, OR
    • It is the largest night ski area in the United States, and the total skiable area encompasses an area of 960 acres (388 ha) (about two thirds of this is lit).
  4. Sundance Mountain Resort, UT
    • Actor Tim Daly broke both of his legs during a skiing accident at Sundance.
    • Actor Robert Redford acquired the area in 1968, and established a year-round resort that would later spawn the independent Sundance Film Festival.
  5. Snowbird, UT
    • Snowbird receives an annual average of 500 inches of low-density Utah powder, attributed to its favorable geography and the lake effect. This phenomenon occurs as northwest winds gather moisture from the Great Salt Lake before ascending Little Cottonwood Canyon and depositing abundant snowfall.
    • Snowbird is the only ski resort in North America with its own tunnel. They call it the Peruvian Tunnel. It opened back in 2005 and spans 600 feet, making it super easy to zip over to Mineral Basin.
